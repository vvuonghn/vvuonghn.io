Vuong Ho is a master’s student in Computer Science at the University of Arkansas, specializing in Computer Vision and Graphics, with a focus on cutting-edge technologies such as Generative Models, Diffusion Models, 3D Gaussian Splatting, and Large Language Models (LLMs). Before pursuing graduate studies, he gained three years of industry experience, where he worked on perception systems for autonomous driving. His contributions included 360-degree view reconstruction from fisheye cameras, camera calibration, 3D vision for object detection and segmentation, and depth estimation.

In addition to his professional background, Vuong’s research has been presented at prestigious conferences such as MICCAI and ACCV. He also holds a patent with the World Intellectual Property Organization, demonstrating his commitment to innovation at the intersection of computer vision and autonomous systems.
